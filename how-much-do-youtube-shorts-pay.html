How Much Do YouTube Shorts Pay in 2026?

YouTube Shorts have become one of the fastest ways for creators to reach millions of viewers. However, Shorts earnings are usually calculated differently from traditional long-form YouTube videos.

Many creators wonder whether Shorts can generate meaningful income. The answer depends on views, audience location, niche and overall monetization performance.

Quick Answer

Most creators earn between $0.01 and $0.10 per 1,000 Shorts views. While individual results vary, Shorts generally produce lower RPMs than traditional YouTube videos.

Why Shorts Pay Less

  • Shorter viewing sessions
  • Different ad revenue sharing model
  • Lower advertiser value per view
  • Higher content consumption volume

Can You Make Money with Shorts?

Yes. Many creators use Shorts to grow their audience quickly and then monetize through long-form videos, sponsorships, affiliate marketing and digital products.

For most channels, Shorts are best viewed as a growth strategy rather than a primary revenue source.
