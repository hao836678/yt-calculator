How Much Does YouTube Pay for 1000 Views in 2026?

Most YouTube creators earn between $1 and $30 per 1,000 views. Actual earnings depend on CPM, RPM, audience location, niche and advertiser demand.

Creators in finance, software, investing and business niches often earn significantly more than gaming, entertainment and meme content.

Quick Answer

If your channel is monetized, 1,000 YouTube views can generate anywhere from $1 to $30 or more. The exact amount depends on the type of audience watching your content and the advertisers bidding for those viewers.

For example, a finance channel targeting viewers in the United States may earn several times more than a general entertainment channel targeting lower CPM countries.

CPM vs RPM

CPM (Cost Per Mille) represents how much advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions.

RPM (Revenue Per Mille) represents the amount creators actually receive after YouTube takes its share of advertising revenue.

Because YouTube keeps a percentage of ad revenue, RPM is usually lower than CPM.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money is 1000 views on YouTube?

Most monetized creators earn between $1 and $30 for every 1,000 views, depending on CPM and audience quality.

Is 1000 views on YouTube good?

For new creators, reaching 1,000 views is an important milestone that indicates growing audience interest.

Which country has the highest YouTube CPM?

The United States, Australia, Canada and the United Kingdom generally have some of the highest CPM rates.

How much does YouTube pay for 1 million views?

One million views can generate anywhere from several hundred dollars to tens of thousands of dollars depending on niche and audience location.

What is the difference between CPM and RPM?

CPM measures advertiser spending, while RPM measures the creator's actual earnings after YouTube's revenue share.
